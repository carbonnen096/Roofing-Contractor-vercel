1. Missing or Damaged Shingles

One of the most visible and urgent signs that your roof may need repair is missing, curled, or cracked shingles. In Wisconsin‚Äôs storm-prone climate, wind and hail can loosen or damage shingles, leaving your roof vulnerable.

What to Watch For:

  • Patches on your roof where shingles are missing.
  • Shingles that are curled upward or appear brittle.
  • Broken pieces of shingles around your home or near downspouts.

Why It Matters:

Exposed roof decking is susceptible to moisture, leading to rot, mold, and eventual water infiltration. During winter, snow accumulation on these weak spots can make matters worse.

2. Granule Loss from Shingles

Asphalt shingles are coated with protective granules that shield them from UV rays and moisture. Over time, these granules loosen and wash away, which is a warning sign of roof aging or storm damage.

Look For:

  • Granules in your gutters or downspouts.
  • Shiny, bare patches on shingles.
  • Discoloration or uneven texture on roof surfaces.

Winter Impact:

Shingles with heavy granule loss can deteriorate faster, allowing ice and snow to penetrate the roof system and cause interior water damage.

3. Water Leaks or Interior Ceiling Stains

If you notice water stains on your ceiling or walls, it's often a sign that your roof has been compromised‚Äîeven if it's not visibly leaking yet.

Signs to Recognize:

  • Brown or yellow stains on the ceiling.
  • Peeling paint or wallpaper.
  • Mold growth in the attic or upper walls.

Cold Weather Risks:

Leaks that go unchecked can worsen in winter due to the freeze-thaw cycle, causing ice to form in gaps and cracks, potentially leading to structural damage.

4. Sagging Roof Deck or Uneven Rooflines

A sagging roof is not just a cosmetic issue‚Äîit usually points to structural problems such as water damage, rotted sheathing, or failing roof supports.

Indicators:

  • Visible dips in the roofline.
  • Sagging ceilings indoors.
  • Cracks near the top corners of walls.

Why Repair is Urgent:

Heavy snow accumulation can overload a weakened structure, increasing the risk of collapse or significant repair costs.

5. Ice Dams and Poor Ventilation

Southern Wisconsin winters often bring freeze-thaw cycles that create ice dams‚Äîthick ridges of ice that build up along the eaves of your roof and prevent melting snow from draining.

What to Look For:

  • Large icicles hanging from gutters.
  • Water dripping from behind fascia boards.
  • Warm attic and cold roof surface‚Äîa sign of poor insulation.

Long-Term Effects:

Ice dams can force water underneath shingles, causing leaks, mold, and even drywall damage. Addressing ventilation and insulation early can prevent this.

6. Damaged or Missing Flashing

Flashing is used around chimneys, skylights, and vents to prevent leaks. If it's rusted, missing, or improperly sealed, it's a high-risk area for water intrusion.

Check For:

  • Rust spots or corrosion on flashing.
  • Separation between flashing and roof.
  • Water stains near chimney or vent openings.

Winter Threat:

Snow and melting ice can quickly enter through unsealed flashing and wreak havoc on your roof‚Äôs interior layers.

7. Moss, Algae, or Lichen Growth

While moss might seem like a minor cosmetic issue, it can hold moisture against the shingles, leading to rot, decay, and premature aging.

Warning Signs:

  • Green moss patches or black streaks on your roof.
  • Damp or dark spots in shaded roof areas.
  • Granule loss beneath mossy regions.

Winter Concerns:

Trapped moisture can freeze and expand, lifting shingles and creating new openings for snowmelt to penetrate.

8. Clogged or Damaged Gutters

Gutters are a crucial part of your roofing system, channeling water away from your home. If they‚Äôre clogged or pulling away from the house, that system fails.

Red Flags:

  • Overflowing water during rain.
  • Sagging or separated gutter sections.
  • Signs of rust or corrosion.

Before Snow Falls:

Cleaning your gutters and ensuring proper drainage can help prevent ice dams, basement flooding, and foundation damage.
